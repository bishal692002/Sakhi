Health Tips

Expert advice for your menstrual health and wellbeing

Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water during your period helps reduce bloating and alleviate cramps. Aim for at least 8 glasses a day.

Heat Therapy

Apply a heating pad to your lower abdomen or lower back to relieve menstrual cramps. The heat helps relax the contracting muscles.

Gentle Yoga

Practicing gentle yoga poses like child's pose, cat-cow, and butterfly can help ease period pain and improve circulation.

Iron-Rich Foods

Combat fatigue during your period by consuming iron-rich foods like spinach, lentils, tofu, and lean meats.

Prioritize Sleep

Getting 7-9 hours of quality sleep can significantly reduce PMS symptoms and improve your mood during your cycle.

Limit Caffeine

Reducing caffeine intake before and during your period can help decrease breast tenderness, anxiety, and insomnia.

Regular Exercise

Engaging in regular moderate exercise can help reduce cramps, boost your mood, and improve overall menstrual health.

Deep Breathing

Practice deep breathing exercises to reduce stress and tension, which can worsen PMS symptoms and period pain.

Magnesium-Rich Foods

Include magnesium-rich foods like dark chocolate, bananas, and nuts to help reduce cramps and improve sleep.

Menstrual Products

Explore different menstrual products like cups, organic tampons, or period underwear to find what works best for your comfort and lifestyle.

Track Your Symptoms

Regularly tracking your symptoms helps identify patterns and empowers you to prepare for and manage your unique cycle experience.

Consider Supplements

Calcium, vitamin D, B6, and omega-3 supplements may help reduce PMS symptoms when combined with a balanced diet.
